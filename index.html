puerto princesa

Carved over millennia by flowing water beneath a lush limestone karst landscape, this UNESCO World Heritage Site and New 7 Wonders of Nature is a subterranean marvel that offers a unique and awe-inspiring journey into the earth’s hidden depths.

boracay, philippines

White Beach is the crown jewel of Boracay Island and one of the most celebrated beaches in the Philippines and the world. Stretching for about four kilometers along the island’s western coast, it is famous for its powdery white sand, clear turquoise waters, and postcard-perfect sunsets.

chocolate hills

Nestled in the heart of Bohol Island, the Chocolate Hills are one of the island’s most iconic natural attractions. This unique landscape of over 1,200 symmetrical mounds, which transform into a rich chocolate hue during the dry season, has long captivated visitors with its surreal beauty and intriguing geological history.

intramuros, manila

Intramuros, Manila’s historic walled city, is a living museum that showcases centuries of Philippine heritage through its preserved Spanish colonial architecture and cobblestone streets.

kayangan lake, palawan

Kayangan Lake in Palawan, is one of the most popular Coron tourist spots and is included in Coron island-hopping tours that depart from town on a regular basis. It is also one of the cleanest lakes in Asia.

kawasan falls, cebu

Cebu is a province in Visayas region of the Philippines that is often recognized as a premier destination. This is so because the island has numerous white sand beaches lining its shore and in islands within its district. Many local and foreign tourists make their way here throughout the year to enjoy the top Cebu tourist spots, learn about its culture and history, and taste its delicious delicacies. Other than the beaches, the province has a number of waterfalls, one of which is Kawasan Falls.

nacpan beach, palawan

Nacpan Beach is a four-kilometer stretch of powdery cream-colored sand lined with swaying coconut trees and clear turquoise waters, located about 17 kilometers north of El Nido town in Palawan Island. Once a hidden gem, Nacpan Beach has grown into one of the top attractions in El Nido Town, attracting beach lovers in search of quiet, unspoiled shores.

read more

calle crisologo, vigan city

Calle Crisologo in Vigan City, Ilocos Sur Province, is one of the most iconic heritage streets in the Philippines. A cobblestone pathway flanked by Spanish-era ancestral homes, vintage lamps, and horse-drawn carriages, this charming road offers a glimpse into the country’s colonial past. As one of the most popular Vigan City tourist spots, Calle Crisologo invites visitors to experience history with every step.
